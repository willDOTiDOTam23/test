Lexus LFA

The Supercar Lexus Built When Nobody Was Looking

A carbon-fiber Lexus that revs to 9,000 rpm, makes 552 horsepower, and was limited to just 500 cars worldwide. If you want a normal supercar, there are easier options. If you want the one that feels like a luxury brand accidentally stumbled into a Formula 1 fever dream and decided to ship it, this is it.

Engine 4.8L naturally aspirated V10
Performance 0–60 mph in 3.6 sec | 202 mph
Built 500 total | hand-assembled

Carbon & Craft

The LFA leans heavily on carbon fiber reinforced polymer, and Lexus developed its own carbon-fiber processes to do it in-house. The result is a cabin that is roughly 100 kg lighter than a comparable aluminum design and still tuned for high rigidity and feedback.

The V10 That Thinks It’s a Race Bike

A 4.8L V10 with a 9,000 rpm redline, a rear transaxle, and lightning-fast automated sequential shifts. Yamaha treated the engine note like an instrument, so the sound you hear is the result of acoustic design, not happy accident.

Nürburgring Package

Only 50 were built with the Nürburgring Package: 562 horsepower, quicker 0.15-second shifts, and track-focused aero including a fixed rear wing. Lexus even bundled Nürburgring driving perks to match the attitude.
